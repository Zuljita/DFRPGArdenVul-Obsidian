# The Archontean Calendar

## Overview
The **Archontean Imperial Calendar**, also called the **Ennian Calendar**, is the primary system used in the eastern region of Magae. It dates back to the signing of the **Compact of Ennius** (**Ennii pactum**). 

- **Years Before the Compact:** *sine pacti* (SP, "without the compact")
- **Years After the Compact:** *ab Ennii pacto* (AEP, "from the compact of Ennius")

### Current Year
The current year is **2993 AEP**, also known as **the 8th year of Emperor Constans XXV**. Official documents reference the reigning emperor's tenure, with imperial offices maintaining lists of emperors and their regnal dates for record-keeping.

## Structure of the Year
- **Total Days:** 372
- **Months:** 12 months of 30 days each (aligned with lunar cycles)
- **Intercalary Periods:** Two additional six-day periods:
  - **Late Summer** (Harvest Festival)
  - **Winter** (Winter Solstice)
- **Weeks:** 7 days, named after significant figures:
  1. **Basilsday** – Named after the Basileus
  2. **Lunday** – Named after the Moon
  3. **Tothsday** – Named after Thoth
  4. **Mitrasday** – Named after Mitra
  5. **Tahsday** – Named after Ptah
  6. **Horasday** – Named after Horus
  7. **Demmasday** – Named after Demma
